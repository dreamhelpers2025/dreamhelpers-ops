# Template — Discovery Questionnaire

**Status:** Standard intake form sent to every prospect before the Day 0 audit call.
**Purpose:** Capture baseline facts asynchronously so the Day 0 call is about interpretation, not information gathering. Reduces audit cycle time and forces the prospect to commit time before we commit ours.
**How to use:** Pick the segment variant (Vet / Insurance / Shopify / Other). Send as a Google Form, Notion form, or shared doc. Target completion 2–3 business days before the Day 0 call.

## Glossary (read once, then skim)

- **Prospect** — the business considering buying an audit. Not yet a client.
- **Segment variant** — Vet / Insurance / Shopify / Other. Each variant changes some questions but the structure is constant.
- **ICP** (Ideal Client Profile) — the type of business we are best positioned to serve.
- **AMS** (Agency Management System), **PIMS** (Practice Information Management System), **Sidekick** — segment-specific operating software; see main glossary in business model.
- **MRR** (Monthly Recurring Revenue) — predictable monthly subscription income.
- **VA** (Virtual Assistant) — overseas part-time admin help.

## SECTION A — About your business (all segments)

A1. **Business name and your role.**

A2. **How long has the business been running?**

A3. **Approximate annual revenue band:**
   - [ ] Under $250K
   - [ ] $250K–$500K
   - [ ] $500K–$1M
   - [ ] $1M–$3M
   - [ ] $3M–$5M
   - [ ] $5M+

A4. **How many employees (including yourself)?** Distinguish full-time, part-time, and contractor (e.g., VA).

A5. **Are you the buyer for this audit, or is it someone else (e.g., your business partner, your spouse, a board)?**

A6. **One-sentence description of what your business does.**

## SECTION B — Your operations today (all segments)

B1. **What does a typical week look like for you?** Best estimate of hours per week across these categories:
   - Customer-facing work (calls, email replies, in-person)
   - Production / service delivery (the actual work you sell)
   - Operations (scheduling, invoicing, follow-up, admin)
   - Marketing / outreach
   - Finance / bookkeeping
   - Strategy / planning
   - Other (specify)

B2. **What's the single workflow that eats the most of your time?** Describe it in 2–3 sentences. (Example: "Responding to 'where is my order' customer emails — I spend ~5 hours/week on this.")

B3. **What's the one operational thing you keep meaning to fix but haven't?**

B4. **What is the latest hour you typically work, on average? Earliest morning?** We're trying to understand where the day already breaks.

## SECTION C — Your tool stack (all segments)

For each, name the specific product if you use one. "We don't have one" is a fine answer.

C1. **Where does your website live?** (Squarespace, Shopify, WordPress, custom, etc.)

C2. **How do customers pay you?** (Stripe, Square, PayPal, manual invoice, etc.)

C3. **What's your email tool?** (Gmail, Outlook, ConvertKit, Mailchimp, Klaviyo, etc.)

C4. **What's your customer / member / patient tracking system?** (Notion, Airtable, HubSpot, Salesforce, your industry's standard, none, etc.)

C5. **What other software do you pay for monthly?** List with approximate monthly cost. (Example: Klaviyo $60, Triple Whale $200, Acuity $30.)

C6. **What software do you use that you HATE?** Why?

## SECTION D — Customer / member touch points

D1. **How many customers / members / patients / clients do you have today?**

D2. **What's the most common reason they reach out to you?** Top 3.

D3. **How do they reach out?** (email, phone, contact form, DM, in-person, etc.) Roughly what percentage by channel.

D4. **What's your average first-response time today?** (Be honest — this is the metric we will improve.)

D5. **How much of your customer service do YOU personally handle vs. delegated?**

## SECTION E — Money flow

E1. **What is your revenue mix?** Rough percentages across:
   - One-time service sales
   - Subscription / membership / retainer
   - Product sales
   - Other

E2. **Where do you have unpaid invoices, dormant subscribers, or pricing you suspect is too low?**

E3. **What was your most recent revenue month? Best month ever?**

E4. **What single workflow change would have the biggest revenue impact, in your gut?**

## SECTION F — AI / automation today

F1. **Are you already using any AI tools?** (ChatGPT, Claude, Sidekick if Shopify, CFSB, Intuit Assist, etc.) For what?

F2. **What's already automated in your operation?** (Even small things — auto-reply emails, scheduled posts, recurring invoices.)

F3. **What's the FIRST thing you'd want automated if it were free and trivial?**

F4. **What's something you would NEVER let AI touch?** (We respect this.)

## SECTION G — Constraints and context

G1. **Are you regulated?** (HIPAA, ABA, SEC, state DOI, TTB, etc.) Briefly describe.

G2. **What's your budget reality for new tools or services in the next 90 days?**

G3. **Are you considering selling the business in 3–5 years?** (Not a sales question — affects what we prioritize.)

G4. **What does success look like from this audit, in your own words?**

## SEGMENT VARIANT — Other (e.g., Soil Detective beta)

Use the universal Sections A–G unchanged. Do NOT use this variant for paid audits unless we explicitly classify the engagement as a beta with custom pricing.

For Soil Detective specifically:
- Add: question about membership tier delivery cadence (which tier benefits run on what cadence)
- Add: question about content production rhythm (videos shot per month, posts published, Patreon updates)
- Add: question about local-community vs. national-audience split

## After the questionnaire returns

1. The auditor reads the entire response. Flags ambiguous answers for the Day 0 call.
2. The auditor produces a **pre-call brief** for the Day 0 call: bullet-point summary of answers, list of clarifying questions to ask, hypotheses about likely pain points.
3. The Day 0 call uses the pre-call brief as the agenda. Do not re-ask questions the questionnaire answered.

# Template — Audit Report Deck

**Status:** Standard 10-slide structure. Built once per audit on Day 4. Presented Day 5.
**Purpose:** The client-facing deliverable for the audit. Doubles as the Implementation proposal — the last 3 slides are the Implementation pitch. This is the artifact the client pays $750 for and the artifact that closes Implementation work.
**Inputs:** workflow map (`02`), pain point map (`03`), opportunity matrix (`04`), discovery questionnaire.
**Output:** a polished deck (Google Slides, Keynote, or PDF) the client receives by end of Day 5.

## Glossary (read once, then skim)

- **Deck** — slide-based presentation. We use Google Slides as the default for shareability.
- **Implementation proposal** — the offer that follows the audit: pick from one of three Implementation tiers (Starter, Standard, Comprehensive) with the audit fee credited.
- **The walkthrough** — the Day 5 call where we present the deck to the client. Script lives in `06-walkthrough-script.md`.
- **Anchor price** — the Implementation tier we present FIRST in the proposal. Usually Standard ($8K) unless scope clearly warrants Starter or Comprehensive.

## The 10-slide structure

Every audit deck has exactly these 10 slides in this order. Slide content varies per client; structure does not.

### Slide 1 — Title / cover

- Client name
- Title: "[Practice / Founder / Owner] Hours Audit"
- Date
- Prepared by Dream Helpers
- Logo

### Slide 2 — What we did

3–4 bullets covering:
- 30-day data review (specify: emails analyzed, tickets reviewed, calendar window, etc.)
- Workflow mapping across [N] major operations
- Pain point inventory and dependency analysis
- AI opportunity scoring with feasibility, time-saved, revenue, and case-study impact

No client-specific findings yet. This slide is method, not output.

### Slide 3 — What we found (executive summary)

5 bullets, no more. The headline findings:

- Where the founder's time is actually going (number, in hours per week)
- Top 1–2 leverage opportunities
- Average finding type: tool reallocation savings, automation candidates, retention engineering, etc.
- One non-obvious finding the client probably did not see themselves
- "Implementation could reclaim [X–Y hrs/week] and protect [$Z–$W/yr]" with explicit ranges (forbid round numbers per §11 Tier B)

This slide is what the client takes a photo of. Optimize for that.

### Slide 4 — Time-use audit

Visualization of the weekly time-block diagram from the workflow map. Two columns:

- **What you told us your week looks like** (stated allocation)
- **What we observed from your data** (inferred allocation)

Highlight the delta. Common pattern: founder thinks they spend 5 hrs on customer service; data shows 12. The slide is the moment they realize where their week actually goes.

### Slide 5 — Bottleneck map

Top 3–5 bottlenecks from the pain point map, in plain language. Each bottleneck:

- One-line name
- One-sentence description of what's happening
- One number: hours/wk OR dollars/yr cost
- One color-coded severity (red / yellow / green)

Pulled from Category B (time-leak) and Category C (asset backlog) pain points primarily.

### Slide 6 — Incumbent AI gap analysis (new in v0.3)

For Shopify, vet, insurance, or other ICPs that have bundled-AI competitors, this slide says:

- What [Sidekick / CFSB / Intuit Assist / their helpdesk's AI] already does for them today
- What they're paying for that the bundled AI now provides for free
- What the bundled AI does NOT do that we will

This slide neutralizes the "but I have Sidekick" objection before it's raised on the call.

For clients with no bundled AI competitor (Soil Detective beta is an example), this slide becomes "AI tools available to you today" — a quick survey of what's in market she could adopt that requires no Dream Helpers work.

### Slide 7 — AI Opportunity Matrix

The opportunity matrix table from artifact 04, simplified to ~8 rows max. Columns:

- Opportunity name
- Feasibility (visual: 1–10 dots)
- Hours saved / week (with range)
- Revenue impact / year (with range)
- Recommended status (lock / off-camera / Wave 2)

Pulled directly from artifact 04 with the audit's Tier B QC already done on every number.

### Slide 8 — Recommended sequence (the case-study locks)

3 Implementation locks, each in its own panel on the slide:

- Lock name
- One-sentence description
- Recommended build duration (2–7 weeks)
- The metric we will measure for the case study

This slide is the bridge from "audit" to "Implementation proposal." The client should mentally start asking "how much?" by the end of this slide.

### Slide 9 — Implementation proposal

Three tiers, with the **anchor tier highlighted** (usually Standard):

| Tier | Price | Scope | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $4,500 | 1 workflow | 3–4 weeks |
| **Standard** | **$8,000** | 2–3 workflows | 4–6 weeks |
| Comprehensive | $12,000 | 4 workflows + dashboard + 30-day tuning | 5–7 weeks |

Below the table:
- Audit fee credit: $750 of today's audit credits toward Implementation if signed within 30 days
- Payment terms: 50% on signature, 50% on UAT completion
- Per-engagement liability cap: equal to fees paid
- Disclosure language: standard (see v0.4 §11)

### Slide 10 — What happens next

3 options for the client, in this order:

1. **Yes, let's go.** Choose a tier. We send the SOW within 24 hours. Implementation kicks off in [N business days].
2. **Yes, but I need to think.** That's fine — the $750 credit holds for 30 days. Here's our calendar to schedule a follow-up.
3. **Not now.** Also fine. Keep the audit deck; the findings are yours. We'll check in at 90 days.

Closes with: contact info, Dream Helpers logo, "thank you."

## Standard appendix slides (optional, depending on client)

After slide 10, optional appendix slides for:

- A1 (workflow map detail) — only if client asked clarifying questions on slide 4
- A2 (pain point full inventory) — only if client wants the full Category A/B/C/D inventory
- A3 (one example Implementation in detail) — only if client wants to see what a build week looks like

Most engagements never use the appendix. Build the deck without it; add only if asked.

## Visual standards

- **Brand:** Dream Helpers brand palette. Lavender primary, cream backgrounds, dark forest text.
- **Font:** Inter for body, Flower Power (Adobe) for slide titles when available.
- **Imagery:** none of the client's competitors' logos. Use generic icons or no icons.
- **Tables / charts:** simple. No 3D, no gradient fills, no logos in tables.
- **Tone:** confident, specific, range-based on numbers, never aspirational. Dream Helpers does not say "could potentially save you up to X"; we say "saves you 8–12 hours per week."

## How to use this template

1. Copy this file into the per-engagement folder as a checklist.
2. Build the actual deck in Google Slides using the structure above. (One sub-template per slide, eventually — for v0.1 we hand-build per audit.)
3. After every slide is drafted, run §11 Tier A QC on the prose.
4. Run §11 Tier B QC on every number, every range, every dollar amount, every hours-per-week claim. Independently re-derive each from the source data. **Forbid round numbers.**
5. Export the final as PDF for client share + keep the Google Slides editable for follow-up edits.
6. Use the [walkthrough script](06-walkthrough-script.md) for the Day 5 call.

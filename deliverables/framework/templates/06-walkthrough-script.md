# Template — Day 5 Walkthrough Script

**Status:** Standard talking-points script + objection-handling cards for the Day 5 audit walkthrough call. Used as-is unless the auditor has explicit reason to deviate.
**Purpose:** Make the walkthrough call repeatable, defensible, and pitched in our actual voice rather than an improvised performance. Increase audit-to-Implementation conversion by walking the client through the deck the same way every time.
**Inputs:** completed audit report deck (`05`), opportunity matrix (`04`), pain point map (`03`).
**Output:** a closed call with one of three explicit outcomes (lock in Implementation, schedule follow-up, decline cleanly).

## Glossary (read once, then skim)

- **Walkthrough call** — the 45-minute Day 5 call where we present the audit deck.
- **Anchor tier** — the Implementation tier we present first in the proposal slide. Usually Standard ($8K).
- **Objection card** — pre-written, practiced response to a specific objection. Forces consistency across founders.
- **Soft close** — a question that gets the client to commit to a next step without pressure ("does Wednesday work for the kickoff call?").

---

## Pre-call (15 minutes before)

The auditor doing the walkthrough does these three things, every time:

1. **Re-derive 3 numbers from the deck independently.** Pick three random numerical claims from slides 3, 4, 5, 7, or 8. Re-derive each from the source data. This is §11 Tier B applied to the live presenter. If any number does not match, fix the deck before the call.
2. **Re-read the discovery debrief.** Refresh on what the client said in their own words. The walkthrough should echo their language.
3. **Pick the anchor tier explicitly.** Before the call starts, decide which Implementation tier (Starter / Standard / Comprehensive) you will present first on slide 9. Default Standard unless scope clearly warrants Starter or Comprehensive.

## The call structure (45 min)

| Minute | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–2 | Open. Thank client for the data, set agenda. |
| 2–6 | Slide 1 + 2. What we did. Brief, no insights yet. |
| 6–12 | Slide 3 + 4. Executive summary + time-use audit. **Pause here for client reaction. This is the moment they realize where their week went. Let the silence sit.** |
| 12–18 | Slide 5 + 6. Bottlenecks + incumbent AI gap analysis. |
| 18–28 | Slide 7 + 8. Opportunity matrix + recommended sequence. **Walk slowly. This is the slide that earns the Implementation.** |
| 28–35 | Slide 9. Implementation proposal. Anchor tier first. Pause for questions. |
| 35–42 | Objections, scope questions, "what would this actually look like?" |
| 42–45 | Slide 10. Three explicit next steps. Soft close. |

## Talking points per slide

### Slides 1–2 (open and method)

> "Thanks again for trusting us with 30 days of your operational data. Over the past 5 days we did three things: we mapped how your business actually runs today, we inventoried where your time goes, and we scored what we could fix with you. That's what's on the next 8 slides."

Do not editorialize on slides 1 and 2. The client is ready to hear findings. Move.

### Slides 3–4 (executive summary + time-use)

> "Here's the headline: [paraphrase the key finding from slide 3 in their own language]. The data backs it up — and what's on slide 4 was the moment we knew it."

Walk slide 4 line by line. The slide shows their stated allocation vs. inferred allocation. **Sit with the gap.** Do not rush to fix it.

> "You estimated [X hours per week] on [activity]. From your [calendar / email / data] we measured [Y hours]. That's [delta] you may not have realized was going there."

After the gap is acknowledged, transition: "So where's the leverage?"

### Slide 5 (bottlenecks)

Walk each bottleneck slowly. For each, ask the client: "Does that match your experience?" Get them to nod or push back on every line. If they push back on a bottleneck, do not defend it — say "OK, we'll de-emphasize that one" and move on. The pushback is information for our Implementation scoping.

### Slide 6 (incumbent AI gap analysis)

The script for this slide depends on whether the client uses bundled-AI products.

**If yes (Shopify with Sidekick, accounting with Intuit Assist, etc.):**

> "You already pay for [Sidekick / Claude for Small Business / Intuit Assist]. Here's what those products do for you today, and here's what they don't. We don't compete with them — we work on top of them."

This pre-empts the "but I already have AI" objection without the client having to raise it.

**If no (Soil Detective beta, most professional services):**

> "There are a few AI tools available to you today that aren't part of our scope. Here's what's worth trying yourself. Here's what makes sense for us to build."

Honesty earns trust. We are not the only AI option, and saying so early signals confidence.

### Slides 7–8 (opportunity matrix + recommended sequence)

This is the slide pair that converts. Slow down.

> "We scored every opportunity on five things: how hard is it to actually ship, how many hours per week it gives back, what it does to your revenue, how visible the result is to your community / customers, and how cleanly it fits with what you already pay for. The full table is here on slide 7. The three things we'd ship first are on slide 8."

For each of the three recommended locks:

> "[Lock name]: this is [one sentence]. We'd ship it in [N weeks]. The metric we'd measure is [specific KPI]. If it works, it gives you back [X–Y hours per week] and protects [$Z range]."

After all three: "If we got all three of these done, you'd be looking at [X–Y hrs/wk reclaimed] and [$Z–W/yr] of new or protected revenue. That's what slide 9 is built around."

### Slide 9 (Implementation proposal)

Anchor tier first. Read it.

> "Standard is $8,000 for [scope summary]. We can ship it in [duration]. Of today's $750 audit, all $750 credits forward if you sign within 30 days, so the net is $7,250."

Then briefly mention the other two:

> "If you want a lighter scope, Starter is $4,500 for one workflow. If you want everything we just talked about and then some, Comprehensive is $12,000 with a 30-day post-launch tuning included. We'd recommend Standard for what we just walked through."

Pause. Let them speak first.

### Slide 10 (next steps)

Read the three options:

1. "Choose a tier. We send you the SOW within 24 hours and we kick off in [N business days]."
2. "Take the deck, think about it, the credit holds for 30 days. We can put a follow-up on the calendar right now."
3. "If now isn't the time, that's fine too — keep the deck, the findings are yours. We'd check in at 90 days."

Then: "Where are you leaning?"

## Objection cards

Each card: when it shows up, what to say, what NOT to say.

### "I already use [Sidekick / CFSB / their AI agent]."

**Say:** "Right — slide 6 covers exactly that. Those products are good at what they do. Here's what they don't do that you're paying for in time today." Walk back to slide 6.

**Don't say:** "Sidekick / CFSB isn't very good." We do not undermine the products they already trust.

### "This seems expensive."

**Say:** "Let's talk about what makes it not. Slide 7 shows ~[X–Y] hrs per week of your time coming back, and [$Z–$W/yr]. At Standard, the build pays for itself in [N months]. If those numbers were wrong, the price would be wrong with them. Where do those numbers feel off to you?"

**Don't say:** "Compared to fractional COOs at $5K/mo we're a bargain." We do not anchor against competitor pricing. We anchor against the value.

### "Can you do something custom outside these tiers?"

**Say:** "Yes, but not in this engagement. We hold the tier structure because it's the thing we can actually deliver reliably in the timeframe. If you want to discuss a custom Comprehensive scope, we can do that — that's the right place for it."

**Don't say:** Yes to anything that's not in the tier structure on the spot. Always defer to a follow-up call for scope variations.

### "I want to think about it."

**Say:** "Totally fair. Let's put a follow-up on the calendar right now — that holds the $750 credit. What does next [Tuesday / Thursday] look like for you?"

**Don't say:** "I'll follow up next week." We schedule the follow-up on the call, on the calendar, before we hang up.

### "Can we start with one piece of Implementation instead of all three?"

**Say:** "Yes — that's Starter at $4,500. Pick the one with the highest impact for you and we ship that first. We can scope a second engagement after Day 30 if it's working."

**Don't say:** "We strongly recommend the full Standard." We respect the budget reality.

### "I want my [partner / spouse / board / accountant] to see this first."

**Say:** "Of course. The deck is yours — share it. Want me to be on the next call when you walk them through it? Often the second pass goes faster if we're on it."

**Don't say:** "Sure, get back to me when you've decided." We position to be on the second call.

### "What if it doesn't work?"

**Say:** "Two answers. First, slide 7's numbers are ranges, not guarantees — we'll measure the actual outcome and report it honestly, good or bad. Second, the SOW includes a per-engagement liability cap equal to fees paid — if we don't ship, you don't keep paying. That's written in."

**Don't say:** "It will definitely work." We do not make outcome guarantees.

## Post-call (within 30 minutes)

1. **Send the deck.** PDF via email + Google Slides link for editable access. Subject line: "[Client name] — Founder Hours Audit + Implementation proposal."
2. **Log the call outcome in CRM.** Status: signed / follow-up scheduled / declined / unknown. Time spent on call.
3. **If signed:** kick off Implementation SOW within 24 hours.
4. **If follow-up scheduled:** confirm the next-meeting calendar invite is sent before end of day.
5. **If declined:** send a one-line thank-you email with the deck attached and a 90-day calendar check-in reminder.
6. **§11 Tier A log:** what worked, what was awkward, what they pushed back on. Feeds template updates.

## QC log

- Pre-call re-derivation: 3 numbers, sources cited.
- Post-call debrief filled within 30 min of hang-up.
